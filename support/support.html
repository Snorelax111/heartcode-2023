FAQs about Deepfake

Tips and Tricks to prevent yourself from being a victim:
  • Use a VPN to mask your IP address
  • Be cautious about the amount of personal information you share online. Minimize the details available publicly, especially on social media platforms.
  • Avoid accepting friend or connection requests from unknown individuals. Masqueraders may use fake profiles to gain access to personal information

  1. Do Not Engage: Avoid responding to or engaging with the content or individuals involved in the cyberbullying. Responding may escalate the situation.
  2. Document and Report: Take screenshots or record instances of the deepfake content. This evidence may be useful for reporting the incident to authorities or online platforms.
  3. Adjust Privacy Settings: Review and update your privacy settings on social media platforms to limit access to your personal information. This can help reduce the chances of further cyberbullying.
  4. Seek Support: Reach out to friends, family, or colleagues for emotional support. Cyberbullying can be distressing, and having a support system is crucial.
  5. Contact Authorities: If the cyberbullying involves threats, harassment, or other criminal activities, consider reporting the incident to local law enforcement. Provide them with the evidence you've collected.
  6. Consult Professionals: If the cyberbullying impacts your personal or professional life, consider consulting with legal professionals or counselors who specialize in online harassment.
  7. Update Security: Strengthen your online security by updating passwords and enabling two-factor authentication on your accounts to prevent unauthorized access.

  1. Contextual Inconsistencies: Consider whether the content aligns with the person's known behavior, habits, or surroundings, and be wary of any inconsistencies.
  2. Inconsistencies in Facial Expressions:Look for unnatural or mismatched facial expressions, especially if they don't align with the context of the content.
  3. Unusual Eye Movements: Pay attention to irregularities in eye movements, blinking, or lack of eye contact, as these can be challenging for deepfake algorithms to replicate accurately.
  4. Artifacts or Glitches: Check for visual artifacts, distortions, or glitches in the video or image that may result from the deepfake generation process.
  5. Lip Sync Issues: Examine whether the lip movements sync properly with the audio, as deepfakes may struggle to match lip movements accurately.
  6. Unnatural Lighting and Shadows: Evaluate the lighting and shadows in the content, as deepfakes may have difficulty replicating realistic lighting conditions.
  7. Abnormal Speech Patterns: Listen for unnatural speech patterns, including robotic or disjointed speech, which may indicate that the audio has been manipulated.
  8. High-Quality Visuals Without Adequate Equipment: If the content appears exceptionally high-quality but is inconsistent with the known recording equipment, it may be a sign of manipulation.
